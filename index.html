History of Computer Science

Bruce Arden

Bruce Arden was an electrical engineer who worked
at the University of Michigan, and was involved in
the creation of the MTS (Michigan Terminal System)
and the revolutionary time-sharing operating system.


Arden began his success by building and programming
IBM's first computer. With this experience under his
belt, he delved into electrical engineering at the
University of Rochester and eventually became a
professor at the university. Arden exclaimed that
computers have evolved faster than he imagined.


"Now, if a computer virus shut down the nation's computers, the whole country would stop. We are that dependent."
